P-N diode and photodiode model

Shows how lifetime and generation change reverse saturation current, dark current, photocurrent, and solar-cell open-circuit voltage.

Reverse saturation current I_S = q A n_i^2 [D_p/(L_pN_D) + D_n/(L_nN_A)]
Photocurrent I_L = J_L A
Solar-cell V_OC V_OC = eta V_T ln(I_L/I_S + 1)
Diffusion lengths L_n and L_p from lifetime and diffusivity

Equations used

I_D = I_S [exp(V/(eta V_T)) - 1]

Dark diode current for the ideal diffusion-limited model.

I = I_D - I_L

Illumination shifts the I-V curve downward by the generated photocurrent.

L_n = sqrt(D_n tau_n), L_p = sqrt(D_p tau_p)

Shorter lifetime shortens diffusion length and raises recombination-limited losses.

n_i(T) rises roughly as T^(3/2) exp[-E_g/(2kT)]

Temperature strongly increases leakage because I_S depends on n_i squared.
